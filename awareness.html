Authentic Medicine Markers

  • Precise, high-resolution printing
  • Dynamic holograms that change when tilted
  • Perfectly aligned security seals
  • Batch numbers match on all packaging
  • Manufacturer contact information present

Counterfeit Warning Signs

  • Blurry or pixelated text and logos
  • Static holograms that don't change
  • Poorly aligned or missing seals
  • Inconsistent batch numbers
  • No manufacturer contact details

Science Behind

Pharmaceutical Authentication Theory

Understanding how genuine medicines are protected and verified

Security Features in Medicine Packaging

Genuine pharmaceutical companies implement multiple layers of security:

  • Overt Features: Holograms, color-shifting inks, embossed logos
  • Covert Features: Microtext, UV markers, chemical taggants
  • Digital Features: QR codes, serial numbers, blockchain tracking
In India, the CDSCO mandates at least 3 security features on all Schedule H drugs.

How Counterfeiters Operate

Common techniques used by fake medicine manufacturers:

  • Refilling: Reusing genuine packaging with fake contents
  • Repackaging: Changing expiry dates or batch numbers
  • Copying: Mimicking packaging with inferior materials
  • Dilution: Reducing active ingredient concentration
78% of counterfeit drugs in India are manufactured domestically (WHO 2022 report).

Counterfeit medicines pose three critical threats:

  1. Health Risks: May contain toxic substances like chalk, arsenic, or road paint
  2. Treatment Failure: Insufficient active ingredients lead to disease progression
  3. Antibiotic Resistance: Sub-therapeutic doses create drug-resistant bacteria

A 2021 Lancet study found fake antibiotics cause 169,000 childhood pneumonia deaths annually.

Modern authentication systems use:

Track & Trace Technologies:
  • Unique serial numbers
  • 2D barcodes (Data Matrix)
  • Blockchain records
Verification Methods:
  • Mobile app scanning
  • SMS verification
  • Web portal checks

India's Drugs Traceability System (DTIS) aims to track 300+ essential drugs by 2025.

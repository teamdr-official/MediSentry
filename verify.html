About Medicine Verification

MediSentry is a comprehensive medicine verification system designed to help consumers identify genuine pharmaceutical products. Our database contains information on thousands of approved medicines from reputable manufacturers.

Verified Medicines

When a medicine is verified, it means it exists in our database and matches the manufacturer's specifications. This indicates the product is likely genuine.

Unverified Medicines

If a medicine isn't found, it could be unlisted, newly launched, or potentially counterfeit. We recommend consulting a pharmacist.

Reporting Suspicious Medicines

Why Reporting Matters

Reporting suspicious medicines is crucial for:

  • Public Safety: Helps authorities identify and remove dangerous counterfeit drugs from circulation
  • Data Collection: Contributes to national databases tracking pharmaceutical fraud
  • Regulatory Action: Provides evidence needed for investigations and legal actions
  • Quality Control: Alerts manufacturers about potential issues in their supply chains

What to Report

You should report medicines that show any of these signs:

  • Unusual packaging or labeling errors
  • Different physical appearance (color, size, texture) from what you're familiar with
  • Unexpected side effects or lack of expected effects
  • Suspicious sources (unlicensed sellers, online pharmacies without verification)
Your reports are anonymous and help protect community health. We take all reports seriously.

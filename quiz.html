What's the most reliable way to verify medicine authenticity?

Which of these is NOT a common security feature on genuine medicine packaging?
Explanation: Blurry barcodes are a sign of poor quality printing often found in counterfeit medicines. Genuine medicines always have clear, scannable barcodes as part of their track-and-trace systems.
3
What unique number is used to track a medicine's manufacturing and authenticity?
Explanation: The batch number identifies when and where the medicine was manufactured, allowing for tracking through the supply chain. This is crucial for verifying authenticity and recalling specific batches if needed.
4
What should you check on the medicine packaging first?
Explanation: You should check all these elements. The expiry date ensures the medicine is still effective, the manufacturer's address verifies its origin, and security features help authenticate the product.
5
Which of these is a red flag for counterfeit medicine?
Explanation: All these are red flags. Genuine medicines have consistent packaging, correct spelling, and always include patient information leaflets. Any deviation could indicate a counterfeit product.
6
What does a tamper-evident seal indicate?
Explanation: Tamper-evident seals show if the packaging has been opened or compromised since leaving the manufacturer. If the seal is broken, the medicine's safety cannot be guaranteed.
7
Where should you buy medicines to minimize the risk of counterfeits?
Explanation: All these are safe sources. Licensed pharmacies, hospital pharmacies, and authorized online pharmacies are regulated and have systems to ensure medicine authenticity.
8
What should you do if the medicine looks different from what you usually take?
Explanation: Always check with your pharmacist if the medicine looks different. While packaging changes do occur, it's important to verify the authenticity and confirm it's the correct medication.
9
What is the purpose of a QR code on medicine packaging?
Explanation: QR codes on medicine packaging serve multiple purposes: verifying authenticity, providing manufacturer information, and often linking to detailed patient information or usage instructions.
10
What should you be suspicious of when buying medicine online?
Explanation: All these are red flags for online pharmacies. Extremely low prices, no prescription requirements, and lack of physical address are common characteristics of illegitimate online medicine sellers.
11
What does a hologram on medicine packaging indicate?
Explanation: Holograms are security features used by manufacturers to indicate genuine products. However, sophisticated counterfeiters sometimes copy holograms, so they should be verified along with other security features.
12
Which organization regulates medicine authenticity in India?
Explanation: The Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) is India's national regulatory body for pharmaceuticals and medical devices, responsible for approving and monitoring medicines.
13
What is the most common type of counterfeit medicine found in India?
Explanation: Lifestyle drugs (for conditions like erectile dysfunction and weight loss) are most commonly counterfeited because they are in high demand and often carry social stigma, making people more likely to buy them from questionable sources.
14
What should you do if a medicine's packaging seems tampered with?
Explanation: You should both return the medicine to the pharmacy and report it to authorities. Tampered packaging could indicate counterfeit or adulterated medicine, which poses health risks and should be reported to prevent harm to others.
15
What should you do if you suspect you've purchased counterfeit medicine?
Explanation: Counterfeit medicines can be dangerous. You should immediately stop using them and report to authorities like CDSCO or state drug controllers. This helps prevent others from being harmed and enables investigations.
